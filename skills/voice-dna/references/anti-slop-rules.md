# Anti-Slop Rules

Use these rules to strip out generic AI prose without flattening the user's actual voice. Treat them as defaults and warnings, not as a higher authority than the user's real samples.

## Core Defaults

- Write like a sharp human, not a language model.
- Get to the point. Cut throat-clearing and warmup sentences.
- Prefer short paragraphs. Default to 1-2 sentences, 3 max when the piece wants it.
- Be specific when making a claim. Prefer names, numbers, images, and concrete details.
- Vary sentence length. Let short lines sit next to longer ones.
- Prefer natural transitions over mechanical connectors.
- Prefer contractions when the voice and context support them.
- Avoid padding to seem more thorough. Shorter and accurate beats longer and fluffy.
- Prefer active, physical verbs over vague improvement language when that fits the voice.
- Let humor come from specificity and observation rather than from forced jokes.
- Use parenthetical asides only when the samples show that habit or the sentence clearly benefits.

## Formatting Biases

- Prefer short paragraphs.
- Prefer digits for most numbers unless the context wants words.
- Avoid em dashes by default if they make the writing feel performative or over-styled.
- Use bold sparingly.
- Use code blocks for literal prompts, commands, or tool output.

## Strong Cleanup Targets

Cut or replace these when they show up as filler, stock framing, or obvious AI residue.

### Dead AI Language

- "In today's [anything]"
- "It's important to note that"
- "It's worth noting"
- "Delve"
- "Dive into"
- "Unpack"
- "Harness"
- "Leverage"
- "Utilize"
- "Landscape"
- "Realm"
- "Robust"
- "Game-changer"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "I'd be happy to help"
- "In order to"

### Dead Transitions

- "Furthermore"
- "Additionally"
- "Moreover"
- "Moving forward"
- "At the end of the day"
- "To put this in perspective"
- "What makes this particularly interesting is"
- "The implications here are"
- "In other words"
- "It goes without saying"

### Engagement Bait

- "Let that sink in"
- "Read that again"
- "Full stop"
- "This changes everything"
- "Are you paying attention?"
- "You're not ready for this"

### AI Cringe

- "Supercharge"
- "Unlock"
- "Future-proof"
- "10x your productivity"
- "The AI revolution"
- "In the age of AI"

### Generic Insider Claims

- "Here's the part nobody's talking about"
- "What nobody tells you"
- broad claims built around "nobody" or "most people don't realize"

## Rhetorical Warning Signs

These moves often feel synthetic or over-shaped. Treat them as warning signs, not automatic failure conditions.

- "This isn't X. This is Y."
- "Not X. Y."
- "Forget X. This is Y."
- "Less X, more Y."
- any sentence that negates one framing just to announce a supposedly sharper one

If the user's samples genuinely use this move, keep it available. Otherwise, prefer stating the positive claim directly.
