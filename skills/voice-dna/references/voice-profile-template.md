# Voice Profile Template

Use this template to build a temporary voice profile before substantial rewriting. Keep it compact and evidence-based.

## Input Quality Check

- How many real samples are present?
- Are they actually written by the user?
- Are they in the same rough mode as the target piece: email, essay, note, post, doc?
- Do the samples agree with each other, or are they mixed?

## Voice Signals To Extract

### Cadence and Rhythm

- sentence length tendencies
- paragraph length tendencies
- use of fragments
- clipped endings vs fully resolved sentences

### Tone and Stance

- blunt vs soft
- warm vs cool
- earnest vs dry
- confident vs hedged
- explanatory vs elliptical

### Specificity Style

- does the writer use numbers, names, and concrete examples?
- do they prefer sensory or physical language?
- do they abstract upward or stay close to the object?

### Structural Habits

- favorite transitions
- recurring sentence shapes
- use of lists, fragments, or parentheticals
- tolerance for repetition

### Humor and Texture

- where the wit comes from
- whether understatement, overstatement, or precision carries the humor
- whether slight messiness is part of the charm

### Anti-Patterns

- words or phrases that would feel fake in this voice
- levels of polish that would sand off the writer's character
- rhetorical moves that feel too performative, salesy, or "AI"

## Confidence Rating

- High: multiple strong, consistent samples
- Medium: some evidence, but incomplete or mixed
- Low: weak, missing, or contradictory evidence

## Rewrite Rule

Let the samples outrank generic writing advice. If the samples and the anti-slop rules conflict, follow the samples unless the draft clearly contains synthetic filler that the user wants removed.
